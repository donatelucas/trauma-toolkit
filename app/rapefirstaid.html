Rape First Aid

If victim is injured or unconscious, contact 911 immediately.

1. Get victim to a safe place and call the police.

2. Do not leave victim alone. Local rape crisis centers can provide additional support and information : call the National Sexual Assault Hotline at 800-656-HOPE (4673), calls will be directed to a local rape treatment center.

3. DO NOT clean up prior to receiving medical attention and rape kit as this leads to destruction of evidence. The victim should not bathe, go to the bathroom, comb their hair, or change clothes until they have received a medical examination. Do not clean up anything at the site of the assault.

4. Get immediate medical attetion. Victim should be tested for STDs and receive emergency contraceptives.

5. Follow up with the victim. Contact a doctor or hotline for references to a support group, therapist, or other resources.
